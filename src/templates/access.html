Accessibility

We are serious about serving the accessibility needs of this community.

If there is any service you need to help your experience at this conference be better, let us know what it is during registration.

We will try our best to make your experience excellent.

Captioning is available upon request.

No accessibility request is too small. We want to hear from you as early as possible.

Diversity

We support the Python Software Foundation's Diversity statement.

Above and beyond this, we are very serious about enabling a diverse conference space which is safe and comfortable for all.

No videotaping, sound recording or photos are allowed unless you get direct permission from the speakers and attendees being recorded. An explanation of why can be found here.

Presentation material containing sexual content is prohibited.

A detailed policy follows, and will also be given to sponsors, staff and security personnel during the conference.

PyGotham is dedicated to providing a harassment-free conference experience for everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion. We do not tolerate harassment of conference participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any conference venue, including talks. Conference participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the conference, without a refund, at the discretion of the conference organizers.

Harassment includes offensive verbal comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, age, sexual images in public spaces, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention. Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

Exhibitors in the expo hall, sponsor or vendor booths, or similar activities are also subject to the anti-harassment policy. In particular, exhibitors should not use sexualized images, activities, or other material. Booth staff (including volunteers) should not use sexualized clothing/uniforms/costumes, or otherwise create a sexualized environment.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the conference organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the conference with no refund. If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact a member of conference or security staff immediately. Conference staff can be identified by special badges.

Conference staff will be happy to help participants contact venue security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the conference. We value your attendance.

We expect participants to follow these rules at all conference venues and conference-related social events.
